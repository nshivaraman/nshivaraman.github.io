Calculating Pressure Loss due to Flow in a Pipe

The diameter of the pipe is D [m]

The length of the pipe is L [m]

The roughness of the pipe is ε [m]

The flow is Q [m^3/s]

The kinematic viscosity is η [m^2/s]

The fluid velocity is v = 4Q/(πD^2) [m/s]

The Reynolds number is Re = vD/η [1]

The Darcy-Weisbach friction factor (1845) is given by the Colebrook-White implicit equation (1939) who developed the equation after experiments by Nikuradse (1933).

An accurate, simplified, explicit approximation of the Colebrook-White equation is the Haaland equation (1983): f=-1.8 log10 ((ε/(3.7D)^1.11+6.9/Re)))^(-2) [1]

The resistance coefficient of the pipe is k = fL/D [1]

The Pressure loss is P = kρv^2/2 [Pa]
