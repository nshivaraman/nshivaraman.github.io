Calculating pressure loss due to flow in a pipe

The flow is Q

The kinematic viscosity is η

The pipe velocity is v = 4Q/(πD^2)

The Reynolds number is Re = vD/η

The Darcy-Weisbach friction factor is given by the Colebrook-White implicit equation (1939)

An accurate, explicit approximation of the Colebrook-White equation is the Haaland equation (1983): f=(-1.8 log10 ((ε/(3.7D)^1.11+6.9/Re)))^(-2)

The resistance coefficient of the pipe is k = fL/D

The Pressure loss is P = kρv^2/2
